02 — A Letter from the Founder

A room that respected the people inside it.

When I set out to build Boaz, I was not trying to build another gym. New York has enough of those. I wanted to build a room that respected the people inside it.

A room where a session was not interrupted, where a sauna did not have a line, where the lighting did not insist on being noticed. A room that was quiet, in the way that good restaurants are quiet — by design, not by accident.

Boaz is built around three commitments: that training is a craft, that recovery is the work, and that community is something you curate, not something you crowd into.

If those things sound familiar, this is your address.

— Boaz
